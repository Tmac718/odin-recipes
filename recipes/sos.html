Creamed Chipped Beef

Quick & Easy S**t on a Shingle

The ultimate comfort food, and a wonderful memory of my childhood. Cream chipped beef or SoS as you may hear it called ("S**t on a shingle") is delicious served over most breakfast foods, such as eggs, potatoes, biscuts or just as enjoyable by itself. The ease of the recipe, few ingredients, and leftovers are just as enjoyable, make this one of my favorite dishes

Creamed chipped beef is a white cream sauce with dried beef added in although it can be substituted for budding meat which is available at most grocery stores. I have also seen recipes using ground or chopped beef, I have not tried this but I'm sure its delicious. This recipe has it served over toast, the bread used is completely your choice. We used whatever was getting stale as kids but my personal favorite is a bagel. So grab a loaf of your favorite bread and lets get cooking.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1-1/2 cups warm milk
  • 1 (8 ounce) jar dried beef
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over low heat. Add flour and whisk until smooth.

  2. Add milk a little at a time, whisking well after each addition.

  3. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and cook, stirring, until thickened.

  4. Stir in beef and cayenne; cook until warmed through.

  5. Toast bread as desired (I prefer mine on a dark toasted bagel)

  6. Place toast on plate, pour creamed chipped beef on top of toast.

  7. Enjoy!
